## Overview

Proves the **DLR identity** for the Yang-Mills measure: for every
finite $\Lambda \subset \mathrm{links}$ and measurable $A$,
$$\mu_{\mathrm{YM}}(A) = \int \gamma(\Lambda, \sigma)(A)\,d\mu_{\mathrm{YM}}(\sigma).$$
This is the formal statement that $\mu_{\mathrm{YM}}$ is a Gibbs
measure for the YM Gibbs specification (`ymGibbsSpec`). The proof
factorizes product Haar via the link-$\Lambda$/$\Lambda^c$ split,
identifies the conditional partition function $Z_\Lambda(\sigma)$
as the inner integral that normalizes $w(\sigma)/Z$ to 1 over
$\sigma_\Lambda$, then uses Fubini to reassemble. The headline
output is `ymMeasure_isGibbs`, which packages the DLR identity into
the upstream `IsGibbsMeasure` structure. Reference: Chatterjee
(2026), §16.3; markov-semigroups `Dobrushin/Specification.lean`.
