## Overview

Verifies that the Wilson lattice Yang-Mills model satisfies Dobrushin's
uniqueness condition at strong coupling. The Dobrushin parameter
$\alpha = \mathrm{maxNeighbors}(d) \cdot (1 - e^{-4n\beta}) =
8(d-1)(1-e^{-4n\beta})$ is shown to be $< 1$ when $\beta <
\tfrac{1}{32n(d-1)}$, which (by the abstract Dobrushin uniqueness theorem)
yields a unique Gibbs measure with exponential correlation decay. Reference:
Chatterjee (2026), §16.3.

## Dobrushin column sum bound

**Lean signature**
```lean
def dobrushinAlpha (d : ℕ) (β : ℝ) : ℝ := maxNeighbors d * influenceBound n β
```
**Informal**: The contraction parameter $\alpha$ used in the YM `DobrushinCondition`: an upper bound on the column sum $\sup_y \sum_x C(x,y)$ of the influence matrix. Equals $8(d-1) \cdot (1-e^{-4n\beta})$. The actual column sum is bounded by this by the chain
$\sum_x C(x,y) \leq \sum_x \mathbf{1}[\text{shares plaq}] \cdot (1-e^{-4n\beta}) \leq \mathrm{maxNeighbors}(d) \cdot (1-e^{-4n\beta})$ (proved in `YMDobrushin.lean`).

## Main result: Dobrushin uniqueness for YM at strong coupling

### [`ym_satisfies_dobrushin`](../../../LGT/MassGap/DobrushinVerification.lean#L190) — Theorem

**Statement**: At strong coupling $0 \leq \beta < \tfrac{1}{32n(d-1)}$, the YM model satisfies the Dobrushin condition: $\alpha < 1$, and there exists a positive mass-gap rate $m > 0$ such that $\alpha^k \leq e^{-mk}$ for all $k \in \mathbb{N}$. Concretely: $\alpha < 1$ via `dobrushin_sufficient`; for the rate, take $m = -\log\alpha > 0$ when $\alpha > 0$ (so $\alpha^k = e^{k \log\alpha} = e^{-mk}$), or $m = 1$ when $\alpha = 0$ (then $\alpha^k = 0$ for $k \geq 1$ and $\leq e^{-k}$).

**Proof uses**: [`dobrushin_sufficient`](../../../LGT/MassGap/DobrushinVerification.lean#L154), [`dobrushinAlpha`](../../../LGT/MassGap/DobrushinVerification.lean#L140), [`influenceBound_nonneg`](../../../LGT/MassGap/DobrushinVerification.lean#L88), `Real.log_neg_iff`, `Real.exp_log`, `Real.exp_nat_mul`, `Real.exp_pos`
