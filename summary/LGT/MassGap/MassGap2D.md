## Overview

Packages the 2D lattice Yang-Mills mass gap as an exponential-decay
statement on the connected 2-point function of plaquette observables:
for any compact gauge group $G \subset U(n)$ and any coupling
$\beta \geq 0$, the bound $|\mathrm{conn2pt}(O_p, O_q)| \leq 4n^2 \cdot
\exp(-m \cdot \mathrm{dist}(p,q))$ holds, with rate $m = -\log(1 -
e^{-2n\beta})$. The proof composes a Doeblin correlation bound
supplied as a bridge hypothesis with the elementary inequality
$(1-\varepsilon)^d \leq \exp(-(-\log(1-\varepsilon)) \cdot d)$.

## The 2D mass gap theorem with connected2pt on the LHS

### [`mass_gap_2d`](../../../LGT/MassGap/MassGap2D.lean#L50) — Theorem

**Statement**: For a compact gauge group $G$ with $\lvert
\mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{tr}(g) \rvert \leq n$, lattice $(\mathbb{Z}/N)^d$, and
coupling $\beta \geq 0$, the connected 2-point function of plaquette
observables decays exponentially in plaquette distance:
$$\bigl|\mathrm{conn2pt}(O_p, O_q)\bigr| \leq 4n^2 \cdot \exp\!\bigl(-(-\log(1 - \varepsilon))
\cdot \mathrm{plaquetteDist}(p,q)\bigr),$$
where $\varepsilon = \mathrm{ymDoeblinLowerBound}(n, \beta) = e^{-2n\beta}$.
The rate $m = -\log(1-\varepsilon) > 0$ is the mass gap. Takes the
Doeblin correlation bound on $|\mathrm{conn2pt}|$ as a bridge
hypothesis (`hBridge`).

### [`mass_gap_2d_rate_pos`](../../../LGT/MassGap/MassGap2D.lean#L100) — Theorem

**Statement**: For $\beta > 0$ and $n \geq 1$, the mass-gap rate
$m = -\log(1 - \mathrm{ymDoeblinLowerBound}(n, \beta))$ is strictly
positive. Equivalently, $0 < 1 - e^{-2n\beta} < 1$, so $\log$ of it
